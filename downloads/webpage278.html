About Tripadvisor

Tripadvisor, the world's largest travel guidance platform*, helps hundreds of millions of people each month** become better travelers, from planning to booking to taking a trip. Travelers across the globe use the Tripadvisor site and app to discover where to stay, what to do and where to eat based on guidance from those who have been there before. With more than 1 billion reviews and opinions of nearly 8 million businesses, travelers turn to Tripadvisor to find deals on accommodations, book experiences, reserve tables at delicious restaurants and discover great places nearby. As a travel guidance company available in 43 markets and 22 languages, Tripadvisor makes planning easy no matter the trip type. The subsidiaries of Tripadvisor, Inc. (Nasdaq: TRIP), own and operate a portfolio of travel media brands and businesses, operating under various websites and apps.

* Source: SimilarWeb, unique users de-duplicated monthly, January 2023

** Source: Tripadvisor internal log files

 

Our Team

Matt Goldberg

President & CEO

Matt Goldberg is President and CEO of Tripadvisor, Inc., the largest travel guidance platform in the world. Matt has more than 20 years’ leadership experience across a diversity of sectors, including travel, digital media, e-commerce, and data, where he’s helped drive innovation and expansion across various businesses. Previously, Matt was Executive Vice President, North America and Global Operations for The Trade Desk, a data-driven global media buying and advertising platform. Before joining The Trade Desk, Matt served as Global Head of M&A and Strategic Alliances and Head of India for News Corp, as well as Senior Vice President, Global Market Development and Head of Corporate Development for Qurate, formerly known as QVC. Prior to that, Matt was CEO of Lonely Planet, a travel guide publisher. Matt currently serves on the Board of Directors of Blue Ocean Acquisition Corp (NASDAQ: BOCNU), and is active in philanthropy and nonprofit leadership, including The Burning Man Project and Lumina Foundation. Matt was also the founding director of Dataphilanthropy, the foundation of The Trade Desk’s founder, dedicated to the mission that passionate, yet data-driven, rational philanthropy is the most effective way to deploy capital against humanity's toughest problems.

Matt holds a Master of Business Administration degree from Stanford University, a Master of Arts in International Studies from The University of Melbourne and a Bachelor of Arts in English from Cornell University.

Matt’s favorite travel destination is Australia, a country where he also lived for six years. 

Michael Noonan

Chief Financial Officer

Mike Noonan is the Chief Financial Officer of Tripadvisor, where he leads global finance, including accounting, financial planning & analysis, investor relations, tax, and corporate development. Mike brings to Tripadvisor over 30 years of experience in capital markets, marketplace and digital subscription models and consumer-focused brands.  

Prior to Tripadvisor, Mike was the Chief Financial Officer of Noom, Inc., a consumer-focused digital health company. Prior to Noom, Mike served as Senior Vice President of Finance for Booking Holdings, Inc., where he led numerous corporate finance activities such as financial planning and capital budgeting, as well as investor relations. Mike’s experience also includes several capital markets roles at RBC Capital Markets, NYSE Euronex, J.P. Morgan, and Bear Stearns & Co.  

Michael holds a Master of Business Administration degree from Duke University and a Bachelor of Arts degree from Davidson College.

Mike's favorite travel destination is London, England. 

Seth Kalvert

Chief Legal Officer and Secretary

Seth Kalvert has responsibility for all of the legal activities of Tripadvisor, Inc. Prior to assuming his role as General Counsel in August 2011, Seth held various positions at Expedia, Inc., most recently as Vice President, Associate General Counsel since February 2006, where he had primary responsibility for the legal aspects of the company's mergers and acquisitions and other transactional work, along with the global Expedia brand, and managed a team that provided legal support for various Expedia brands, including Tripadvisor and Hotwire, in addition to privacy and trademarks across Expedia, Inc. Before joining Expedia, Seth held various legal positions at IAC/InterActiveCorp, which he joined in July 2001. Previously, Seth held a business development role at a privately-held online social networking and e-commerce company and practiced law in the New York office of Debevoise & Plimpton, LLP. Seth holds an AB from Brown University and a JD from Columbia Law School.

Seth's favorite travel destination is Queenstown, New Zealand.

Sugata Mukhopadhyay

Chief Technology Officer

Sugata is the Chief Technology Officer at Tripadvisor, responsible for leading the company’s engineering organization worldwide. With over 15 years of experience at Tripadvisor, Sugata has touched nearly every area of the business. As head of engineering for Tripadvisor’s Rentals and Experiences business units, Sugata has overseen several significant technical projects that have helped scale the business. Sugata continues to lead engineering for the Experiences & Rentals unit in addition to his global information technology role that spans across all of Tripadvisor globally. Prior to joining the Tripadvisor team, he was involved with several early stage startups across media, FinTech and mobile technology verticals, both as a founder and in engineering leadership roles. Sugata holds undergraduate and graduate degrees in Computer Science from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur and Cornell University.

Sugata's favorite travel destination is Iceland.

Sanjay Raman

Chief Product Officer

Sanjay Raman is the Chief Product Officer for the Tripadvisor Core business, responsible for product development serving both travelers and partners on the Tripadvisor website and app. Prior to his role at Tripadvisor, Sanjay was  Director of Product Management for Airbnb, where he led product and cross-functional teams to improve the quality of trips across the platform. Earlier in his tenure, he led the growth of Airbnb Plus, a premium traveler offering, where he was responsible for scaling the supply of Plus homes while creating a high-quality experience for guests.

Prior to Airbnb, Sanjay was Vice President of Product at Houzz, a leading online platform for home remodeling and design. While in the role, he built and scaled the highly successful Houzz Marketplace business, which continues to enable consumers and professionals to buy home goods through the company’s platform. 

Previously, at Greylock, Sanjay worked with and led investments in fast-growing consumer internet companies, with a focus on marketplaces and new media businesses. Earlier in his career, Sanjay co-founded and grew two successful companies and held product management roles at Google. 

He holds both Bachelor of Science and a Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

His favorite travel destination is the Lofoten Islands in Norway.

Kristen Dalton

Chief Operating Officer

Kristen Dalton is the Chief Operating Officer for the Tripadvisor Core business, responsible for strategy development and operational planning and execution. Prior to this role, Kristen Dalton served as Vice President of Finance for the Tripadvisor Group and interim head of the brand’s B2C business segment, leading the company’s guidance, experiences, flights, vacation rentals and direct-to-consumer revenue lines. In this role, Kristen led the strategic, financial and operational planning function for the entire Tripadvisor Group.

Prior to Tripadvisor, Kristen served as the Vice President of Finance & Strategy for Vistaprint, Inc., where she oversaw a global team providing leadership to strategy and internal communications teams and leading financial planning and analysis functions. Notably, Kristen led the work to better align product, marketing and technology and Vistaprint’s strategic planning processes to drive operational improvements to achieve important strategic outcomes. 

Previously, Kristen was Vice President of Strategy - International Life for Ace Group (now Chubb), a global leader in commercial and personal property and casualty (P&C) insurance. While at the Ace Group, Kristen drove the strategic planning process for a $1 billion global business unit and international life insurance portfolio, partnering with the C-suite and finance team to create a centralized strategic planning framework to unify individual regions following multiple acquisitions.  

Kristen also served in senior leadership roles for AXA, a multi-national insurance provider, Zurich Financial Services, a global life insurance provider, and Houlihan Lokey Howard & Zukin, a leading investment bank.

Kristen holds a Bachelor of Science in Accounting from Villanova University.

Her favorite travel destination is New York City.

Kanika Soni

Chief Commercial Officer

As Chief Commercial Officer, Kanika is responsible for all Tripadvisor B2B revenue, across Hotels, Vacation Rentals, Experiences and Restaurants, as well as growing the company’s media solutions and advertising business. Kanika has extensive experience in scaling digital and e-commerce businesses while keeping customer experience at the core of business strategy.

Kanika joined Tripadvisor from The Walt Disney Company in April 2019, where she led global e-commerce for the Consumer Products Division. During her tenure at Disney, she helped turn the business around to deliver sustained double-digit revenue growth. This transformation centered around several strategic initiatives including driving increased traffic, expanding product assortment and improving the shopping experience. Prior to Disney, Kanika held leadership positions at Tesla Motors, Gilt Groupe and McKinsey & Company. Kanika holds an MBA from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business and a Bachelor's degree in Business Administration from Delhi University.

Kanika’s favorite travel destination is Cartagena, Colombia.

Ben Drew

President, Viator

 

Ben leads Viator, a Tripadvisor company and the world’s largest tour and activity booking site. Under Ben’s leadership, Viator gives travelers access to incredible experiences around the world, and helps tour operators grow their audiences, bookings and businesses.

As head of Viator, Ben also oversees Bokun, the Iceland-based and globally-leading software company serving the tours, attractions and experiences industry. Bokun was acquired by and integrated into Tripadvisor in 2018 under Ben’s leadership.

Ben joined Tripadvisor as head of business development for vacation rentals in 2012, where he led significant inventory growth, and the acquisitions of vacation rental brands Niumba, VacationHomeRentals and HouseTrip. Prior to joining Tripadvisor, Ben led business development for emerging markets at Expedia, and worked as a strategy consultant at Deloitte. Ben holds a degree in economics from the University of Cambridge. 

Ben’s favorite travel destination is anywhere above 14,000 feet. 

Almir Ambeskovic

CEO, TheFork

Almir Ambeskovic is the CEO of TheFork. At this role he’s responsible for setting TheFork’s business strategy and leading global operations of the brand, inclusive of TheFork’s operations in 22 countries around the world, to continue the mission to bring happiness through amazing and accessible dining experiences. Prior to being appointed as CEO, Almir was Vice President Sales and Marketing for TheFork, a role in which he was responsible for the growth of the business after driving as Regional Director the leading markets of TheFork (Italy and France among others) for several years. He joined TheFork in 2014 as Italy Country Manager following the acquisition by Tripadvisor of RestOpolis, an online restaurants reservation startup he founded and led since 2011 in Milan. Almir began his career as a consultant and entrepreneur in the IT sector. First as start-up entrepreneur founding three companies, then as top manager of a digital scale-up as TheFork, Almir has always been active as a mentor for innovation in Italy. In 2012, Almir created and hosted the first Italian television program on start-ups and innovation. He was also vice president of the group of young entrepreneurs of Assolombarda for six years until 2016.  

Almir's favorite travel destination is Sicily, Italy (though for dining out he loves Rome, "the eternal city" and home of the delicious Carbonara).
