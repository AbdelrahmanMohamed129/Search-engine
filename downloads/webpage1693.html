    Frequently Asked Questions

    What are the differences between each Ring Video Doorbell?

    All Ring Video Doorbells can send notifications to your phone, tablet and PC when anyone presses your doorbell or triggers the built-in motion sensors. When you answer the notification, you can see, hear and speak to visitors from anywhere. The main differences between each doorbell are their additional features and how they receive power.

    Video Doorbell is powered by a rechargeable, built-in battery or can be hardwired to an existing doorbell system. It features 1080p HD video and Customisable Motion Zones that let you create and adjust your own Motion Detection areas. (reducing unnecessary motion notifications and conserving the battery life of your Video Doorbell).

    Battery Video Doorbell Plus includes Head-to-Toe View, which gives you an expanded view from your door, so you can see more of people and packages. 1536p HD Video gives you a clearer picture of what's happening and you can see it all after dark with Colour Night Vision. Powered by a Quick Release Battery Pack, Battery Video Doorbell Plus also features Two-Way Talk, Advanced Motion Detection, Customisable Privacy Settings and much more.

    Video Doorbell 3 is powered by a rechargeable Quick Release Battery Pack for easier charging or can be hardwired to an existing doorbell system. It has all the features of Video Doorbell 2, plus Dual-Band wifi at 2.4GHz and 5GHz (vs 2.4GHz only on Video Doorbell 2) for enhanced connectivity, and Customisable Motion Zones that let you create and adjust your own Motion Detection areas (reducing unnecessary motion notifications and conserving the battery life of your Video Doorbell 3). Video Doorbell 3 also has improved screw strength, a redesigned easy-to-remove faceplate, and automatic chime connection, which makes installing your Video Doorbell easier than ever.

    Video Doorbell 4 is powered by a rechargeable Quick Release Battery Pack for easier charging or can be hardwired to an existing doorbell system. It has all the features of Ring Video Doorbell 3, plus Colour Pre-Roll technology, which allows you to see up to 4 seconds of colour video before the motion event was even triggered.

    Door View Cam is powered by a rechargeable, Quick Release Battery Pack. Unlike other doorbells, which are typically mounted to the side of a door, Door View Cam can be mounted directly on your door to replace a compatible peephole. It also features 1080p HD video, Knock Detection, and Customisable Motion Zones.

    Video Doorbell Wired connects to your existing doorbell wiring for always-on power, and protection when you need it most. Featuring Live Video and Two-Way Talk, to let you see, hear and speak to whoever is at your door, from wherever you are. Set it up in the Ring app, add a Chime, or connect to an Alexa-enabled device, and you'll always know when someone drops by.

    Video Doorbell Pro connects to existing doorbell wires for nonstop power. It features 1080p HD video, Dual-Band Wi-Fi, a slim design and customisable motion zones that let you create and adjust your own motion detection areas.

    Video Doorbell Pro 2 features 1536p HD Head to Toe Video, Two-Way Talk, 3D Motion Detection, Bird’s Eye View, enhanced dual-band wifi, and Customisable Privacy Settings. Available with a Ring DIN Rail Transformer for hardwired installation using existing doorbell wiring, or with Ring Plug-In Adapter which powers Video Doorbell Pro 2 from a standard electrical socket.

    Video Doorbell Elite uses Power Over Ethernet to connect to the internet and receive power. Ideal for new or remodeled homes, Video Doorbell Elite has a flush mount and a sleek design with customisable motion zones and 1080p HD video.

    Do Ring Doorbells save my videos and photos so I can review them later?

    If you have a subscription to Ring Protect, videos captured your doorbell will be saved to your Ring account for up to 180 days (default storage set to 30 days), so you can review them at any time.

    Go here​ to learn more about Ring Protect.

    Photos captured will be saved to your Ring account for up to seven days. All Ring Video Doorbells come with a free 30-day trial of Ring Protect.[1] During or after a trial, you can choose to purchase a Ring Protect plan to save your videos and photos. Photo capture is currently available on these devices.

    Can I share my videos and photos with people?

    If you have a subscription to Ring Protect, you can choose to share your videos and photos with anyone, including neighbours, friends, family and local police. All Ring Doorbells come with a free 30-day trial of Ring Protect. You can choose to subscribe to Ring Protect at any time to save, review and share all videos and photos captured by your Video Doorbell, and you won’t be charged until the end of your free trial.

    Go here​ to learn more about Ring Protect.

    What’s a Ring Protect Plan?

    Ring Protect Plans are optional offerings that let you get the most out of your Ring devices. There are two tiers of Ring Protect Plans: Basic and Plus.

    The Ring Protect Basic Plan activates video recording, photo capture and sharing for individual Ring Doorbells and Cameras. It saves all your videos to your Ring account for up to 180 days (default storage set to 30 days) and photos for up to 7 days, so you can review and share your videos at any time. Ring Protect Basic Plans start at only 3.99 € a month per device.

    The Ring Protect Plus Plan adds even more to your home security. It includes video recording, photo capture and sharing for unlimited Ring Doorbells and Cameras at your home. Ring Protect Plus Plans start at only 10,00 € a month per home.
